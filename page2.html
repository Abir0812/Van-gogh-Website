This section examines some of Vincent van Gogh's most significant paintings, analyzing their artistic techniques, historical context, and cultural importance.

Perhaps van Gogh's most famous work, "The Starry Night" was painted during his stay at the Saint-Paul-de-Mausole asylum. The painting combines observation with imagination, featuring a view from his window with an added village that he painted from memory.

The swirling patterns in the sky demonstrate van Gogh's interest in movement and energy, while the cypress tree in the foreground creates a dramatic vertical element that contrasts with the horizontal village below.
